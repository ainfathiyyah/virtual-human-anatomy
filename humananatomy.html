Skeletal System

The skeletal system consists of 206 bones that support body structure, protect vital organs, produce blood cells and assist movement.

Main Bones & Functions

  • Skull : Supports body structure and posture.
  • Vertebral Column : Protects the spinal cord and supports the head.
  • Rib Cage : Protects the heart and lungs.
  • Pelvis : Supports the upper body and provides attachment points for leg muscles.
  • Femur : The longest and strongest bone in the body, supporting the weight of the upper body.

Interesting Facts

  • Adults have 206 bones.
  • Hands and feet contain over 100 bones.
  • The femur is the longest bone.

Importance in Daily Life

The skeletal system helps humans stand, walk, run and perform everyday activities. Without a healthy skeletal system, body movement and protection of internal organs would be difficult.

Muscular System

The muscular system contains more than 600 muscles responsible for movement, posture, balance and body heat production.

Main Functions

Types of Muscles

  • Skeletal Muscle
  • Smooth Muscle
  • Cardiac Muscle

Major Muscles & Functions

  • Biceps – Helps bend the elbow and lift objects.
  • Triceps – Straightens the arm and supports pushing movements.
  • Deltoid – Controls shoulder movement and arm rotation.
  • Abdominal Muscles – Support posture and protect internal organs.
  • Quadriceps – Assist walking, running and jumping activities.

Interesting Facts

  • The human body contains over 600 muscles.
  • The gluteus maximus is the largest muscle.
  • The heart is a specialized muscle.

Organ System

Organ systems work together to maintain life, regulate body processes and ensure proper body function.

Major Organs & Functions

  • Brain – Controls body activities and processes information.
  • Heart – Pumps blood throughout the body.
  • Lungs – Provide oxygen to the blood and remove carbon dioxide.
  • Stomach – Digests food and absorbs nutrients.
  • Liver – Processes nutrients, detoxifies substances, and produces bile.
  • Kidneys – Filter waste products from the blood and produce urine.

How Organs Work Together

The brain controls body activities, the heart pumps blood, the lungs provide oxygen, and the digestive system supplies nutrients. Together, these organs maintain life and body balance.

Interesting Facts

  • The brain contains about 86 billion neurons.
  • The heart beats over 100,000 times daily.
  • The liver can regenerate itself.
